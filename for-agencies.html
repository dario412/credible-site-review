How agency briefs work

Not one creator
at a time — programmes.

Agency briefs look different from direct brand briefs. You're typically building a multi-creator programme — three voices across a content series, a speaker roster for an event calendar, an ambassador and a supporting cast for a category campaign.

The brief shape is closer to a media brief than an influencer brief. Multiple creators, multiple formats, multiple touchpoints across a quarter or a year. The commercial structure needs to work for your client, for us, and for you.

We're set up for that. We scope multi-creator programmes as a single engagement with one contract, one point of contact, and one reporting framework — not twelve separate freelancer deals you have to stitch together.

Partnership models

Three ways to work together.

White-labelled, co-credited, or fully behind the scenes. The structure depends on how you want to show up to your client.

Model 01

Talent supply

You bring the client relationship. We supply the talent, the negotiation and the delivery. You wrap it in your process, brand and reporting.

Best for: agencies with established client delivery but no B2B creator roster.

Model 02

Co-delivery

Joint pitch, joint delivery. Two logos on the deck, one shared scorecard. Best for large brand programmes where the split of work is clear.

Best for: multi-pillar programmes that need both creative agency and talent management.

Model 03

Referral

You pass us the work when it doesn't fit your roster or model. We handle the client, revenue share back to you. Standard terms, no drama.

Best for: agencies who get occasional B2B expert requests outside their core.

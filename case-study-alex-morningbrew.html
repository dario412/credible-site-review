01

The challenge

Post-Morning Brew, Alex faced the problem most successful media founders face after an exit: enormous latent demand and no infrastructure to service it. Brands were queuing up with sponsorship briefs. Events had him on their top-of-list. Podcast partnerships were flying in cold-DM.

Traditional talent bureaus wanted to book keynotes only. Traditional PR agencies didn't understand the creator economics. Alex wasn't a talking head — he was a media business, and he needed representation that understood the difference.

02

Our approach

We started by auditing every existing commercial commitment and channel — the newsletter, the podcast, the LinkedIn engine — and building a quarterly business plan against the four-revenue-line model.

The first six months focused on saying no. We closed out one-off sponsorships that didn't fit the positioning, killed a speaking calendar that was eating prep time without commercial return, and rebuilt the content calendar around three brand partnership formats we controlled.

Once the base was clean, we scaled: three flagship ambassadorships, one major recurring content series, and a keynote programme with higher fees and stricter format constraints.
